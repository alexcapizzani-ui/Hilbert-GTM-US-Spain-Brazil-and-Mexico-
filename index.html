Hilbert · Target Map

Who Hilbert should target, by market

Each retailer gets a signal score out of 100 for how learnable its customer data is, plus a separate decision-locus tag for whether that market's team can actually close the deal. Score and locus are independent: a great-data account run from another country still needs the right team on the call.

Scale 0–40

real data Revenue. Basis differs by market, see method.

Frequency 0–35

estimated From category: grocery 35, beauty 26, fashion 14.

Identity 0–25

estimated From loyalty/membership model: 25, 13 or 5.

Locus tag

Where decisions get made. Not part of the score.

How the score is built

Add three components for a total out of 100. Scale is real revenue data; frequency and identity are estimated from category and business model.

Scale · from real revenue
Over $1B online revenue (or $100B+ US total retail sales)40
$250M–$1B online (or $30B–$100B US)32
$50M–$250M online (or $10B–$30B US)24
$10M–$50M online (or $3B–$10B US)16
Under $10M online (or under $3B US)8
Frequency · estimated from category
Grocery (buy every few days)35
Convenience / fuel (very high visit frequency)32
Beauty / pharmacy (roughly monthly)26
Fashion (a few times a year)14
Electronics / DIY / home / telecom8
Identity · estimated from business model
Strong: loyalty card, membership, store credit, marketplace login25
Some: basic account or email capture, no real program13
Weak: mostly anonymous checkout, no card at all5
A = 70 and above B = 45 to 69 C = below 45
Worked example. Walmart US: scale 40 (over $100B total retail sales) + frequency 35 (grocery) + identity 25 (Walmart+, marketplace) = 100, tier A. Aldi US: scale 32 + frequency 35 but identity only 5, because Aldi runs no loyalty card, total 72, still A but weaker on the dimension that matters most for Hilbert.

Decision locus, and why it's separate from the score

A high score means the data is rich. It says nothing about whether the local team can actually sign the deal. Zalando scores well on data, but its decisions are made in Berlin, so it is not a workable Spain-market target even though it appears on the Spain list.

Local: HQ and decisions in this market Centralized elsewhere: foreign parent decides Regional or distributed: pan-region or cooperative/franchise
